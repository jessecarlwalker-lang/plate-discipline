About

The Hidden Game

Baseball is an artform. Its medium is physics. Every pitch carries spin axis, velocity, extension, movement — invisible forces that produce every drama the camera captures. The home run that wins October. The strikeout that ends a career. The swing adjustment that saves one. All of it governed by forces the naked eye never sees.

We make them visible.

What This Is

Plate Discipline publishes visual essays — data-driven, designed for the browser, built to be felt as much as read. We pull Statcast data, build visualizations in code, and fuse them with writing that respects what you already know. Every piece is a single, self-contained argument where the numbers earn their place and every scroll reveals something new.

This is not a blog with charts bolted on. This is the data and the story woven into one thing. The understanding is the experience.

Who This Is For

You already know what xwOBA means. You understand pitch sequencing. You care about the microscopic margins — two degrees of launch angle, three inches of break — that shift league-wide trends. You've read the smart analysis elsewhere, and the presentation was flat. You've watched the beautiful highlights elsewhere, and they said nothing. You want both at once, designed to be felt together. That's what we build.

Jesse Walker

Jesse Walker writes about baseball through data. He played center field in high school, found his real position behind a spreadsheet, and has been pulling threads on the game's hidden mechanics ever since.
